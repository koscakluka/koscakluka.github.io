# 1. Burnout Definition
---

World Health Organization (WHO) in the 11th Revision of International
Classification of Diseases (ICD-11) defines burnout as:
 
> Burnout is a syndrome conceptualized as <u>resulting from chronic workplace
> stress</u> that has not been successfully managed.
> 
> It is characterised by three dimensions:
> 
> 1) feelings of energy depletion or exhaustion;
> 2) increased mental
> distance from oneâ€™s job, or feelings of negativism or cynicism related to one's
> job; and
> 3) a sense of ineffectiveness and lack of accomplishment.
> 
> Burn-out refers specifically to <u>phenomena in the occupational context</u>
> and should not be applied to describe experiences in other areas of life.


# 2. The Three Dimensions of Burnout
---
<br />

<div class="grid grid-cols-1 grid-rows-[repeat(9, auto)] md:grid-cols-3 md:grid-rows-[auto_auto_auto]">
      <div class="md:order-1">**Exhaustion**<br/>*(Physical and Emotional Dimension)*</div>
      <div class="md:order-4">
      Physical symptoms:
      - Chronic fatigue
      - Sleep disturbances
      - Susceptibility to illness
      - Digestive problems
      - Headaches
      </div>
      <div class="md:order-7">
      Mental symptoms:
      - Difficulty concentrating
      - Forgetfulness
      - Decision-making challenges
      - Mental fog
      </div>
      <div class="md:order-2"><div class="md:hidden pt-4" />**Cynicism**<br/>*(Interpersonal Dimension)*</div>
      <div class="md:order-5">
      Behavioral indicators:
      - Withdrawing from colleagues
      - Minimizing work involvement
      - Using derogatory language
      - Treating clients/colleagues as objects
      </div>
      <div class="md:order-8">
      Emotional indicators:
      - Detachment from work identity
      - Loss of enjoyment
      - Persistent negative attitude
      - Compassion fatigue
      </div>
      <div class="md:order-3"><div class="md:hidden pt-4" />**Inefficacy**<br/>(Self-evaluation Dimension)</div>
      <div class="md:order-6">
      Performance impacts:
      - Decreased productivity
      - Lower confidence
      - Lack of achievement
      - Poor self-evaluation
      </div>
      <div class="md:order-9">
      Career implications:
      - Reduced career satisfaction
      - Impaired professional development
      - Limited advancement opportunities
      - Increased likelihood of career change
      </div>
</div>


# 3. Six Factors of Burnout
---


<div class="grid grid-cols-1 md:grid-cols-[auto_1fr] md:grid-rows-[auto_auto_auto]">
<div>**Workload**</div>
<div>
Assessment Criteria:
* Quantity of work
* Complexity of tasks
* Time pressure
* Resource availability
* Recovery opportunities

#### Warning Signs:
* Regular overtime
* Missed breaks
* Work spillover into personal time
* Constant deadline pressure
* Insufficient resources

#### Interventions:
* Workload audit tools
* Time-blocking techniques
* Resource allocation reviews
* Delegation strategies
* Priority management systems
</div>
<div>**Control**</div>
<div>
#### Assessment Criteria:
* Decision-making authority
* Resource access
* Schedule flexibility
* Process input
* Goal setting participation

#### Warning Signs:
* Micromanagement
* Rigid procedures
* Limited input in decisions
* Restricted autonomy
* Inflexible schedules

#### Interventions:
* Autonomy assessment
* Decision-making frameworks
* Flexible work arrangements
* Employee empowerment programs
* Feedback mechanisms

</div>
<div>**Reward**</div>
<div>
#### Assessment Criteria:
* Financial compensation
* Recognition systems
* Career advancement
* Learning opportunities
* Work-life benefits

#### Warning Signs:
* Uncompetitive pay
* Limited recognition
* Stagnant career path
* Few development opportunities
* Inadequate benefits

#### Interventions:
* Recognition program audit
* Career pathway mapping
* Skills development planning
* Benefits review
* Performance reward alignment

</div>
<div>**Community**</div>
<div>
#### Assessment Criteria:
* Team dynamics
* Communication patterns
* Support systems
* Conflict resolution
* Collaboration quality

#### Warning Signs:
* Poor team cohesion
* Communication breakdown
* Isolation
* Unresolved conflicts
* Limited peer support

#### Interventions:
* Team building initiatives
* Communication workshops
* Support group formation
* Conflict resolution training
* Collaboration tools
</div>
<div>**Fairness**</div>
<div>
#### Assessment Criteria:
* Policy transparency
* Resource distribution
* Opportunity access
* Decision fairness
* Workload balance

#### Warning Signs:
* Perceived favoritism
* Unclear policies
* Inconsistent treatment
* Hidden opportunities
* Unfair workload distribution

#### Interventions:
* Policy review process
* Transparency initiatives
* Equal opportunity programs
* Fair workload distribution tools
* Decision-making frameworks
</div>
<div>**Values**</div>
<div>
#### Assessment Criteria:
* Mission alignment
* Ethical congruence
* Purpose fulfillment
* Cultural fit
* Practice-value alignment

#### Warning Signs:
* Value conflicts
* Ethical dilemmas
* Purpose disconnect
* Cultural misalignment
* Practice-value gaps

#### Interventions:
* Values assessment
* Purpose alignment workshops
* Ethics training
* Cultural fit evaluation
* Practice-value alignment tools
</div>
</div>
